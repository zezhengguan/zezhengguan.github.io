![RoboScope](/images/projects/RoboScope.png)

RoboScope is a modular pin-array display that allows users to interact with and visualize volumetric transformations of an urban landscape. The system relies on an array of motorized "3D-Pixels" that voxelize urban massing into a physical, touchable form, with each 3D-Pixel currently supporting two modes of interaction: pulling and pushing.

Building on these foundations, the project was later incubated under the name PinScope through an MIT-led collaboration with MIT Media Lab, benefiting from in-depth discussions with MIT faculty and researchers on its technical, social, and urban implications. This phase explored further applications of large-scale pin-array displays as spatial interfaces for community visioning, urban simulation, and iterative decision-making. Following the visit, PinScope was exhibited for three months at The 10th Bi-City Biennale of Urbanism/Architecture (UABB, Shenzhen), marking an important step in translating academic research into public-facing urban discourse.
