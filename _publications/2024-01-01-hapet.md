Recent research shows that virtual pets offer companionship and emotional support, while effective haptic feedback is crucial for immersive interactions, enhancing these benefits. We are interested in encountered-type haptic displays, which provide direct feedback by positioning a tangible surface for user interaction. While current encountered-type haptic displays focus on flat surfaces, little research explores their use on dynamic and curved surfaces. Most virtual pet studies emphasize visual feedback and 2D screen-based haptics, with limited exploration of direct interaction through shape-changing displays in VR. To bridge these gaps, we introduce HaPet, a hemispherical desktop-level encountered-type haptic display designed to allow users to freely explore curved surfaces. To explore its effectiveness and usability, we developed a virtual pet application and conducted a user study comparing HaPet with both the VIVE controller and a static HaPet. This comparison was aimed at evaluating how well these devices support user immersion and enjoyment during virtual pet interactions. The results of the study indicate that the dynamic haptic feedback provided by HaPet significantly enhances the user's sense of immersion and enjoyment in VR, outperforming both the VIVE controller and the static HaPet.
